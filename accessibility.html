Accessibility statement

Last updated: 9 May 2026

Cybespoke is committed to making cybespoke.co.uk as accessible as possible. We believe a well‑built website should work for everyone, regardless of how they read, navigate or interact with it.

Our standard

We aim to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, level AA — the standard recommended by the UK government and the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). We design and develop our own marketing pages and our clients' projects against the same standard.

What we do

  • Use semantic HTML and a logical heading structure on every page.
  • Provide visible focus styles so the website is fully usable with a keyboard.
  • Maintain sufficient colour contrast and avoid conveying meaning by colour alone.
  • Add descriptive text alternatives for meaningful images.
  • Ensure forms and interactive components are labelled and announce changes to assistive technology.
  • Support browser zoom up to 200% without loss of content or functionality.
  • Respect the prefers‑reduced‑motion setting and avoid animation that flashes or scrolls aggressively.

Conformance status

This website is partially conformant with WCAG 2.1 AA. "Partially conformant" means that some parts of the content do not yet fully meet the standard, but we are actively working to resolve them.

Known limitations

We are aware of, and working to fix, the following:

  • Some decorative animations on hero sections may exceed comfortable motion levels for some users. We honour prefers‑reduced‑motion, but we are continuing to refine the threshold.
  • A small number of older custom illustrations may benefit from richer text alternatives.

If you spot anything else that prevents you from using the site, please tell us — see below.

Compatibility

This website is designed to work with current versions of the following browsers and assistive technologies:

  • Chrome, Edge, Firefox and Safari (current and previous major versions).
  • iOS Safari and Chrome on Android (current and previous major versions).
  • VoiceOver, NVDA, JAWS and TalkBack screen readers.

You may notice reduced functionality on browsers more than two major versions out of date.

Reporting an accessibility issue

If you encounter a barrier or have any difficulty using this website, please tell us so we can fix it. Email hello@cybespoke.co.uk with a short description of:

  • What you were trying to do.
  • Where on the site the problem occurred (a URL is helpful).
  • The browser, device and any assistive technology you were using.

We aim to acknowledge accessibility reports within one working day, and to provide a more detailed response within five working days. Our hours are 09:00 – 17:00, Monday to Friday (UK time).

How this statement was prepared

This statement was last reviewed on 9 May 2026. It is based on our own internal testing using a combination of automated tools, manual keyboard testing, and screen‑reader checks across the browsers listed above.

Enforcement

If you contact us with a complaint about accessibility and you are not satisfied with our response, you can contact the Equality Commission for Northern Ireland, which is responsible for enforcing relevant accessibility legislation in Northern Ireland.
