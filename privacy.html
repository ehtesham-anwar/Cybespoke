This notice explains how Cybespoke ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses and protects your personal information when you visit our website, contact us, or work with us as a client. We are committed to handling your information transparently and in line with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018 and the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR).

1. Who we are

Cybespoke is a bespoke software studio operating remotely from Belfast, United Kingdom. We act as the data controller for any personal information described in this notice.

You can reach us at hello@cybespoke.co.uk. Our usual response hours are 09:00 – 17:00, Monday to Friday (UK time), excluding public holidays.

2. Information we collect

We only collect what we genuinely need. Depending on how you interact with us, this may include:

  • Enquiry information — your name, email address, organisation, the message you send us, and any project details you choose to share through our contact form, our project brief form, or by email.
  • Client information — if you become a client, we may also process invoicing details, contractual contacts, and information you provide for the purpose of delivering the work commissioned.
  • Technical information — when you visit our website we automatically collect a small amount of technical data (such as pages viewed, approximate location derived from your IP address, browser type and referrer). Where possible we use privacy‑friendly analytics that do not identify individual visitors.
  • Cookies and similar technologies — see our Cookie policy for full details.

We do not knowingly collect special category data (such as health or biometric data). Please do not send us this information unless we have explicitly agreed it is necessary for a project.

3. How we use your information

We use your information to:

  • Reply to your enquiry and prepare a proposal or quote.
  • Deliver the bespoke work you have engaged us to provide.
  • Issue invoices, take payment and meet our accounting obligations.
  • Operate, secure and improve our website.
  • Comply with our legal and regulatory obligations.
  • Send occasional service updates to existing clients (you can opt out at any time).

We do not sell your information, and we do not use it to send unsolicited marketing.

4. Our lawful bases

Under the UK GDPR we rely on the following lawful bases for processing your information:

Replying to enquiries and quoting for workSteps taken at your request prior to entering a contract

5. Who we share your information with

We share your information only with carefully selected service providers who help us run the studio. These typically include:

  • Our email and productivity provider (for correspondence and document storage).
  • Our hosting and domain providers (for serving this website).
  • Privacy‑respecting analytics tooling (where consent has been given).
  • Our accountant and payment processor (for invoicing and tax).
  • Source‑control, project‑management and design tools used to deliver client work.

Each provider is bound by a written agreement that requires them to handle your information in line with the UK GDPR. We may also disclose information where required by law, regulator request, or to protect our legal rights.

6. International transfers

Some of our service providers are based outside the United Kingdom — typically in the European Economic Area or the United States. Where information is transferred outside the UK, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as the UK International Data Transfer Agreement, the UK Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses, or an adequacy decision recognised by the UK government.

7. How long we keep your information

  • Enquiries that do not become projects — kept for up to 12 months, then deleted.
  • Client project records — kept for the duration of the engagement and for up to 7 years afterwards, in line with UK accounting and limitation periods.
  • Invoicing and accounting records — kept for at least 6 years, as required by HMRC.
  • Website analytics — kept in aggregated form for up to 24 months.

8. Your rights

Under UK data protection law you have the right to:

  • Be informed about how your information is used.
  • Request a copy of the information we hold about you.
  • Ask us to correct information that is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Ask us to delete information in certain circumstances.
  • Restrict or object to certain processing.
  • Request a portable copy of information you have provided to us.
  • Withdraw any consent you have given, at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, please email hello@cybespoke.co.uk. We will respond within one calendar month.

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your information you can also complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk. We would, however, appreciate the opportunity to put things right first.

9. How we protect your information

We use sensible, proportionate technical and organisational measures to keep your information safe. These include encryption in transit, access controls, regular software updates, and secure backups. No system is ever entirely secure, but we take our responsibilities seriously and review them regularly.

10. Children

Our website and services are not directed at children under the age of 16, and we do not knowingly collect information from them.

11. Changes to this notice

We may update this notice from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or the law. The "last updated" date at the top of this page will always show when the notice was last revised. Material changes will also be highlighted on this page.

12. Contact us

For any questions about this notice or your personal information, please email hello@cybespoke.co.uk. We aim to respond within one working day.
